# Vaultline — 3-minute demo script

Spark hackathon submission · solo. Target runtime **2:50–3:00** (judges cut at 3:00 —
better to land at 2:55 than run over). ~300 words of narration + screen actions.

---

## Pre-flight (do this BEFORE you hit record)

- **Demo on testnet, name-drop mainnet.** Do the live clicking on the **testnet** app
  (free MON, instant, no real loss on camera). Show the **mainnet** contract + verified
  badge for the "it's real" proof at the end. Never risk real MON or a slow mainnet
  confirmation on a live take.
- **Seed the calendar first.** On the demo wallet, pre-create a commitment and report a
  few days so the streak calendar already shows **green cells + at least one red** — a
  blank calendar is a dead screen.
- **MetaMask**: pre-connected, on the right network, funded, unlocked.
- **Tabs pre-opened** so nothing loads on camera: `/` , `/dashboard`, `/v/<your-address>`,
  and the mainnet contract on monadscan.
- **Record 1080p, dark room.** The phosphor theme glows on a dark capture.
- **One risky moment**: the tx confirmation. Either pre-warm it, or keep a backup take of
  just the slash animation to cut in if the wallet lags.

## The script

### 0:00 – 0:28 · Hook  *(camera on you or the landing page)*

> "I trade gold — XAUUSD — on prop-firm accounts. And here's what nobody tells you: the
> firm doesn't blow your account. **You** do. Two losses in, you're revenge trading. You
> said three trades a day, max — you're on your sixth. Every trader has rules. Almost
> nobody has consequences for breaking them. A trading journal has no teeth. So I built
> one that does."

### 0:28 – 0:48 · What it is  *(landing page)*

> "This is Vaultline. You stake MON, you commit to your own trading rules, and every day
> you report whether you kept them. Clean day, your streak grows. Break a rule — or just
> go quiet — and it slashes part of your stake. A commitment contract for discipline,
> live on Monad."

### 0:48 – 1:30 · Create a commitment  *(the "/" create page)*

> "You start here. Check off your rules — max daily loss, max trades, session hours only,
> no revenge trading — or write your own. They get hashed **in your browser** — only the
> hash goes onchain, so your strategy stays private but you can always prove what you
> promised."

*(scroll to stake/terms; fill stake, duration, slash %)*

> "Then the terms: how much MON, how many days, and how hard each violation bites. I
> stake, sign… and it's onchain."

### 1:30 – 2:15 · The desk + the slash  *(/dashboard)*  ← **the money moment**

> "This is the desk. Every day of the commitment is a cell — green if I was clean, red if
> I slipped. Stake, streak, days left — all read straight off the chain, no server, no
> database."

*(hover the "Report Today" section)*

> "Now the honest part. Say today I broke my rules. I hit **Violated** — and before I
> confirm, it tells me exactly what it'll cost…"

*(confirm in the dialog; let the slash land)*

> "…and there it goes. Real MON, slashed, gone. Miss a day entirely and it counts the
> same — going dark is not an exit."

### 2:15 – 2:42 · The thesis  *(stay on dashboard)*  ← **the intellectual core**

> "Now you're thinking: nothing checks my trades — I could just lie. **Exactly.** There's
> no oracle. It's self-reported on purpose. You can lie to it, same as you can lie to a
> journal — the difference is now lying costs you real money. It's the cheapest honesty
> you'll ever buy."

### 2:42 – 3:00 · Close  *(/v/<address>, then flash the verified mainnet contract)*

> "Every vault has a public page — your streak, shareable, provable. And this is live on
> Monad **mainnet** right now — contract verified, fully non-custodial, no admin key.
> It's called Vaultline. Your word, your stake."

*(end on the wordmark or the public streak page)*

## Director's notes

- **Pacing**: the script is deliberately ~300 words so you have room to breathe and let
  clicks land. Don't rush the slash — that half-second of MON disappearing is the whole
  pitch. Silence there is good.
- **If you're running long**, cut the create-page detail (0:48–1:30) down to one line —
  the dashboard slash and the thesis are non-negotiable; everything else is support.
- **The emotional arc**: relatable pain (0:00) → simple mechanic (0:28) → see it bite
  (1:30) → the clever twist that answers the obvious objection (2:15) → it's real (2:42).
  Judges remember the twist. Land it clean.
- **No invented numbers.** Don't claim users, returns, or "X% more disciplined." The
  honesty thesis is the differentiator — leaning on fake metrics undercuts it.
- **Backup**: keep a screen-recording of a full create→report→slash cycle saved locally.
  If anything hangs live, cut to it and keep narrating.
